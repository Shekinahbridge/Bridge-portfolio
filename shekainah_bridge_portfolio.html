Featured Project

Jill Fashions & Textiles

Flutter mobile app case study · Personal project · In progress

Jill Fashions & Textiles is a mobile app I designed and developed to help fashion designers move away from handwritten record books and manage customer orders more easily on a phone. I created the app for my father, who is a fashion designer, so the project comes from a real workflow and a real need.

My role UX/UI designer and developer
Built with Flutter, Dart, Firebase
Status Currently being finalized

The problem

Many fashion designers still record customer details, measurements, and order timelines in physical books. That process can be difficult to manage over time, especially when trying to search for old records, follow up on active orders, or keep customer information organized in one place.

The goal

I wanted to create a mobile experience that makes order recording feel simple, structured, and accessible. The aim was to help a designer quickly add customer details, view active orders, track dates, and reduce dependence on paper records.

My design approach

I focused on a calm interface with a soft color palette, simple task-based navigation, and screens organized around the daily work of a fashion designer. Instead of making the experience feel technical, I wanted it to feel familiar, friendly, and easy to learn.

Why this project matters to me

This project made me realize how much I enjoy the design process. Thinking through how the user should move from screen to screen, what information should be visible first, and how to reduce friction during tasks is what pulled me more strongly toward UX/UI design.

Home Screen Quick access to key tasks such as adding orders, viewing orders, and settings.
New Order Flow Structured form for customer details, order dates, measurements, and photos.
Settings Preference controls including notifications, dark theme, and gallery-saving options.
Order Management Searchable order list designed to make customer records easier to review and retrieve.

What I Learned

Designing for a real user changes everything

Because the app was created for my father, I had to think beyond appearance and focus on practicality. That pushed me to consider clarity, routine tasks, and how the interface could support someone who needs speed and ease of use in everyday work.

UX/UI is where I feel most engaged

Building this project helped me see that the part I enjoy most is shaping the user experience: organizing information, creating a visual system, and designing screens that make work feel easier.
